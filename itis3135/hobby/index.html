Who Invented Camping?

Thomas Hiram Holding is widely regarded as the father of modern camping, a pioneer whose love for the great outdoors revolutionized the way people experience nature. Born in 1844, Holding's passion for camping began at an early age, sparked by a significant 1853 journey across the United States with his parents, which included camping in the wild. This early adventure laid the foundation for his lifelong dedication to promoting camping as a form of leisure and exploration. He later crossed the prairies of America in a wagon train, an experience that deeply influenced his views on outdoor living and self-reliance, further fueling his desire to share these experiences with others.

Holding's contributions to the world of camping were both practical and influential. In 1908, he published "The Campers' Handbook," which quickly became the definitive guide to camping and outdoor activities at the time. This handbook covered everything from setting up camp and cooking in the outdoors to advice on clothing and equipment, effectively codifying camping as a popular leisure activity. He went on to establish the Association of Cycle Campers in 1901, which is now known as The Camping and Caravanning Club, one of the largest and oldest camping organizations in the world. Through his writings, advocacy, and the establishment of this club, Thomas Hiram Holding inspired countless individuals to explore and appreciate the natural world, leaving an indelible mark on the culture of outdoor adventure and camping.
